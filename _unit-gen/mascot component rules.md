# Bots & Bones — Mascot Component Rules

Use these nine customizable parts when composing mascot unit prompts.

## 01 — Display / Face
Controls the pixel expression, display state, eye language, loading symbols, diagnostic symbols, and emotional tone.

Rules:
- Keep the same display shape and pixel-face rendering style.
- Do not replace the face with a human face.
- Expressions must stay minimal, readable, and mascot-like.

## 02 — Head Attachments
Controls headphones, antennas, sensors, caps, helmets, top modules, and small head-mounted devices.

Rules:
- Attachments must fit the original head proportions.
- Do not crop attachments.
- Keep silhouettes compact unless the prompt explicitly asks for a technical module.

## 03 — Body Shell / Material
Controls shell material, transparency, internal visibility, surface finish, panels, and material swaps.

Rules:
- Preserve the original body proportions and volume.
- Use only the Bots & Bones brand palette.
- Prefer translucent plastics, smoked acrylic, matte ABS, technical-gray panels, and premium electronics finishes.

## 04 — Front Controls / Buttons
Controls button icons, button labels, small display indicators, controls, knobs, and embedded symbols.

Rules:
- Icons must look physically embedded into the existing controls.
- Keep icons minimal and readable.
- Do not add large UI panels over the body.

## 05 — Storage Slots / Cavities
Controls visible compartments, cartridge slots, memory slots, connector bays, and stored objects.

Rules:
- Objects must sit inside the compartment and not protrude too far.
- Keep compartments clean and believable.
- Avoid overcrowding tiny cavities.

## 06 — Arms / Hands
Controls arm markings, hand pose details, grippers, wrist modules, handheld tools, and small interaction props.

Rules:
- Preserve the original arm shape and pose.
- Props must match mascot scale.
- Do not hide the hands with oversized objects.

## 07 — Modules / Accessories
Controls detachable modules, cartridges, sensors, cameras, cables, probes, badges, tools, and unit-specific accessories.

Rules:
- Accessories should clarify the selected unit type.
- Keep accessories modular and collectible.
- Do not turn the mascot into a different character.

## 08 — Stickers / Labels / Markings
Controls operational labels, serial numbers, status ticks, arrows, QR-like marks, tape labels, and microtypography.

Rules:
- Markings must be sparse and intentional.
- Use brand colors only.
- Keep text/labels small, technical, and product-like.

## 09 — Wear / Finish / Status Lights
Controls dust, scratches, smudges, operational wear, reflections, glow, ready/error indicators, and final surface polish.

Rules:
- Wear must stay subtle and premium.
- Status lights must use accent-red #ff3b30 and accent-green #49f7a5 only.
- Do not make the robot look destroyed, dirty beyond use, or post-apocalyptic.
