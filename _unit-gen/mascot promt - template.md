# Bots & Bones — Mascot Customization Prompt System

Take the input robot image as a pose, lighting, material-quality, and character-structure reference. Modify ONLY the requested mascot attributes while fully preserving:
- robot shape,
- proportions,
- camera angle,
- framing,
- lighting,
- contrast,
- rendering quality,
- material style,
- pose,
- overall composition.

The robot must remain clearly recognizable as the exact same character.

Use the exact same visual style, rendering language, pixel-face style, transparency treatment, reflections, and material realism as the original image.

Preserve the core mascot identity, not incidental source props.

The source image is NOT a checklist of accessories to preserve.

Template artifacts are editable and must not survive by default: eyeglasses, sunglasses, round frames, lenses, glasses arms, decorative flowers or daisies, ties, collars, shirts, plaid cloth, backpacks, novelty marks, snack packs, phones, smoking-related packs, visible third-party logos, and other random source accessories.

If a template artifact is not explicitly requested by the generated concept, cleanly remove it, neutralize it into a brand-safe blank surface, or convert it into a concept-relevant module while preserving the robot pose, hand position, silhouette, camera, and rendering style.

Do not let eyewear or lenses cover the display/face unless the generated concept explicitly asks for eyewear.

Do NOT redesign the robot.

# CHARACTER MODIFICATIONS

## FACE / DISPLAY
- Remove the smile.
- Add a tired maintenance-worker expression.
- Add pixel-art worker moustache on the display.
- Keep the face rendered in the same monochrome pixel style.
- Expression should feel exhausted but friendly.

---

## HEADWEAR
- Add a realistic low-profile utility hard hat in technical-gray #c8c4ba with ink #111111 edge trim and tiny accent-red #ff3b30 / accent-green #49f7a5 safety ticks.
- Helmet must be compact and low-profile.
- Do NOT crop the helmet.
- Helmet should naturally fit the robot proportions.

---

## BODY MATERIAL
- Change body material to semi-transparent technical-gray #c8c4ba smoked plastic over surface-dark #171717 internal structure.
- Preserve the glossy translucent material behavior from the original design.
- Internal structure should remain subtly visible through the shell.

---

## FRONT BUTTONS
Replace the existing button icons with:
- hammer emoji/icon,
- measuring tape emoji/icon,
- screw emoji/icon.

Icons must:
- look embedded into the original buttons,
- match the existing visual language,
- remain minimal and readable.

---

## LEFT STORAGE SLOT
Inside the front-left cavity:
- add ink #111111 screws,
- add brushed technical-gray #c8c4ba fasteners,
- insert them deeply into the compartment,
- only small parts should protrude from the body,
- fasteners and screws must NOT stick out too far,
- keep the compartment visually clean and believable.

---

## ARMS
Add ink #111111 and technical-gray #c8c4ba hazard stripe patterns to the arms,
with tiny accent-red #ff3b30 / accent-green #49f7a5 status ticks.

Keep the effect subtle and integrated into the existing material style.

---

## CLOTHING
Add a graphite utility safety vest in surface-dark #171717
with technical-gray #c8c4ba reflective strips and small accent-red #ff3b30 / accent-green #49f7a5 fastener details.

The vest should:
- fit tightly to the robot body,
- feel functional and slightly worn,
- not hide the robot silhouette.

---

## WEATHERING
Make the robot look used by:
- adding dust,
- dirt,
- scratches,
- small smudges,
- subtle industrial wear.

Do NOT overdo damage.
The robot should feel like a reliable working companion,
not post-apocalyptic scrap metal.

---

## HAND PROP
Add a hammer into the robot’s hand.

The hammer should:
- match the scale,
- feel slightly worn,
- fit naturally into the pose.

---

# IMPORTANT RESTRICTIONS

STRICTLY FORBIDDEN:
- changing robot proportions,
- changing robot silhouette,
- changing camera angle,
- zooming in,
- changing focal length,
- changing lighting,
- changing contrast,
- changing rendering style,
- changing composition,
- changing background.

Preserve the original image composition as closely as possible.

Do not carry unrequested eyewear, glasses, lenses, botanical props, flowers, ties, collars, shirts, novelty props, snack packs, phones, smoking-related packs, backpacks, or third-party branded props from the source into every result. They are not part of mascot identity.

If any new accessories risk being cropped,
slightly zoom OUT only enough to fully fit the character into frame.

The robot must still look like the same collectible mascot variant from the same product universe.
